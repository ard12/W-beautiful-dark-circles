STANDING OPERATIONAL DOCTRINE — THEATER ALPHA

Force Protection: All units maintain minimum 30% resource levels across fuel, ammunition, and medical supplies. Any unit falling below 30% on any resource is flagged for immediate resupply consideration. Units at 0% on any critical resource are classified as degraded and should not be tasked for forward operations without replenishment.

Escalation Thresholds: Threat scores above 70 require immediate commander notification and an active response recommendation. Scores above 85 authorize pre-positioned CAS assets to prepare for engagement without additional approval. Scores below 40 are monitored but do not require active response.

Medical Evacuation: MEDEVAC requests take priority over logistics resupply. The nearest medical-capable unit should be identified and routed within the current operational window. Casualty evacuation routes must avoid active threat axes.

Close Air Support: CAS requests require commander approval. Digital 9-line submissions are preferred. CAS assets on station may be engaged against confirmed hostile targets when threat score exceeds 85 and friendly forces are within 2km of the threat axis.

Resupply Windows: LOGPAC convoys should be routed during periods of reduced threat activity. Convoy routes must avoid the primary threat axis. Hotel-series logistics units maintain resupply authority for all forward-deployed elements.

Rules of Engagement: Positive identification required before engagement. Proportional response mandated. All engagements must be logged with timestamp, target description, and outcome for after-action review.
